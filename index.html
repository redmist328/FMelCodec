Ultra-Low-Bitrate Mel-Spectrogram-based Neural Speech Coding with Flow-Matching-based Refinement and Vocoding-driven Reconstruction

Hui-Peng Du, Xiao-Hang Jiang, Yuan Tian, Yang Ai, and Zhen-Hua Ling

Abstract

Ultra-low-bitrate speech coding is crucial for bandwidth-constrained communication, yet preserving naturalness and speaker traits under extreme bit budgets remains difficult due to severe information loss and unstable quantization. To this end, we propose FMelCodec, an ultra-low-bitrate neural speech codec in the mel-spectrogram domain, formulated as a three-stage coding–refinement–reconstruction (CRR) framework: the coding stage compresses mel-spectrograms using a ConvNeXt v2 encoder–decoder with a single 1024-entry VQ codebook and an effective waveform-level compression factor of 640, achieving 250 bps at 16 kHz (and 750 bps at 48 kHz), while an online clustering strategy dynamically relocates underutilized codewords to mitigate codebook collapse in the single-codebook regime; the refinement stage employs a lightweight conditional flow matching (CFM) model to enhance the codec-degraded mel-spectrogram, and we further introduce a self-consistency training scheme for CFM to improve refinement quality under few-step inference; finally, the reconstruction stage uses a HiFi-GAN vocoder to synthesize waveforms from the refined mel-spectrogram. Experiments on LibriTTS-16k and VCTK-48k show that FMelCodec achieves better or comparable results to strong neural codec baselines under the same ultra-low-bitrate constraint, offering a favorable rate–quality–complexity trade-off.
